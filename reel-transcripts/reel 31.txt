Layer 13, availability and recovery. This is the one you don't think about till 2 a.m. Availability means your app is up when users need it, which is 24 7 365. Recovery means you can get it back up when it goes down and trust me it will go down. Murphy's Law. Servers crash, databases corrupt, and deploys break things all the time. So the question isn't if it's going to go down, it's how fast you can recover. So here's your minimum setup. First, automated database backups. Supabase has this on by default. Neon does it continuously. And if you're self-hosting, schedule backups and send them to cloud storage. That's a win. And don't forget to test your restores because a backup you've never restored might not even work. Second is uptime monitoring. Use a free tool that pings your site every five minutes and texts you when something goes down. You should never, ever find out your app is down from one of your users.
