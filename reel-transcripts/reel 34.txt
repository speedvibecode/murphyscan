Your database has 10 million rows. Queries that took 20 milliseconds now take 4 to 5 seconds. Adding indexes is not fixing it anymore. Your single Postgres instance has hit its ceiling. Here are three things you can do right now to fix it. Step 1. Shard by tenant. If you built multi-tenancy with an org ID column, you already have natural shard key. Each large tenant gets their own database. Small tenants share a pooled instance. Superbase lets you spin up isolated projects per tenant. Route at the application layer based on the org ID. Step two, use Citus for transparent sharding. Citus extends Postgres with distributed tables. You pick a distribution column, usually tenant ID or user ID. Citus handles routing queries to the right shard automatically. Your application code does not change. Same SQL, same ORM, data lives on multiple nodes.
