## Why It Matters
A product can launch technically and still leak users because new signups never reach value. Activation and retention are launch controls when the business depends on repeat use, paid conversion, or user habit formation.

## Verification Questions
- Has the product identified the one core action that predicts retention?
- Is that action tracked for each new user in the first session or first 24 hours?
- Is the path to the aha moment measured by steps, elapsed time, and percentage of signups reaching it?
- Are users who fail to return within 48 hours flagged for follow-up?
- Are activation nudges, emails, in-app messages, or contextual tooltips wired to real behavior?
- Is activation drop-off treated as a launch-readiness risk rather than only a growth metric?

## Agent Notes
Use this for products where signup volume matters but retention, activation, or conversion evidence is missing. Do not apply it as a blocker to static tools, internal admin surfaces, or one-shot utilities unless the product promise depends on repeat engagement.
